Review of Yuwen's Plot of Passenger Flows on the D Line

Reviewer: Nick Jones (nj935)

Comment: This is an extremely interesting and data-rich plot. The analysis behind it is very cool and interesting. The use of color and the inclusion of a specific set of gridlines is good presentation. What's really cool is that the subway stations are shown in linear form just like in reality, ie. northern stations at the top and southern stations at the bottom.

It's great because it's unusual, and I haven't seen it before, but it tells an interesting story in graphical terms. The presentation is well done including use of white text over red bars (for negative values). Data is portrayed without distortion.

I have one recommendation for improvement, which is to make sure any graphic like this passes the '0.5 second test', ie. can a guy on the street tell what the graphic's about, and what the key message is, within 0.5 seconds. Or equivalently, if you put it on the pages of the New York times, would the message be clear instantly. With some fine-tuning this could achieve that, and it's worthwhile because it's a sophisticated piece of data viz.

Possible ways to increase the immediacy of comprehension:

  • Add a headline in narrative form: eg. "New York's D train has several stations where the number of passengers disembarking is consistently and disproportionately high" ... or whatever it may be. By narrative form, that would be a sentence with a subject, verb and object, in active tense - basically the construction of sentence that you use if you're a headline writer for the New York Times.
  • Label the time period more clearly
  • Consider a more intuitive unit than percentage of net passenger flow. Could it be ratio passengers boarding to passengers disembarking?

Overall verdict: Super cool and interesting plot.
